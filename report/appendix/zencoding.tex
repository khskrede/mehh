\section{Z-Encoding}
\label{zencoding}

The External-core identifiers are z-encoded. Meaning they use "z" and "Z" as prefixes
for certain symbols:

\begin{tabular}{ | l | l |}
\hline
Character 	& Code	\\
\hline
Tuples:		& 	\\

(\#\#)		& Z1H	\\
()		& Z0T	\\
(,,,)		& Z3T	\\

\hline
Constructors:	&	\\
(		& ZL	\\
)		& ZR	\\
{[}		& ZM	\\
{]}		& ZN	\\
:		& ZC	\\
Z		& ZZ	\\
\hline
Variables:	&	\\
z		& zz	\\	
\&		& za	\\
|		& zb	\\
\^{}		& zc	\\
\$		& zd	\\
=		& ze	\\
>		& zg	\\
\#		& zh	\\
.		& zi	\\
<		& zl	\\
-		& zm	\\
!		& zn	\\
+		& zp	\\
'		& zq	\\
\textbackslash	& zr	\\
/		& zs	\\
{*}		& zt	\\
\_		& zu	\\
\%		& zv	\\
c		& znnnU	\\
\hline
\end{tabular}
